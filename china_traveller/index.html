5 Places I highly recommend you visit when you get there

Harbin

Tiger leaping gorge

yangshuo

shanghai

emeishan

Home to the snow and ice city

Located in Heilongjiang province, this northeastern city sits on the border with Russia. The key draw to Harbin is the snow and ice festival that they host every winter. Other fun things to explore are the Russian architecture, food and frozen lakes you can walk across.

A sense of adventure

Hiking the Tiger Leaping Gorge will take you on a beautiful hike through Yunan province. If you're particularly fast and fit, it's possible to complete the hike in one day. However, my recommendation would be to stay at one of the many hostels that are at the half-way point and complete the hike over two days.

true freedom

Yangshuo can be accessed via a short train from Guilin. The small town has a limited in-terms of restaurants and places to stay. However, it more than makes up for this in scenery. To get the full experience, I rented a scooter and spent days exploring all Yangshuo had to offer. Topped off with multiple hikes.

china's melting pot

Easily the most well-known location on my list. However, I felt I couldn't leave it off. Shanghai is one of the best cities I've ever visited, a truly modern metropolis which offers a unique diversity of things to eat and do compared to other major cities in China.

a voyage of transcendence

One of the four holy Buddhist mountains in China, Emeishan is a unique experience. Accessible by train from Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan, how you start your hike is entirely up to you. I took a taxi to a reasonable starting point, made my way to the cable cars and hiked the remainder of the way. It is possible to scale the entire mountain from stem to stern, but this will require multiple days, so come prepared.
